Clinical Experience

Visiting Medical Student Mallinckrodt Institute of Radiology During my time in the Interventional Radiology (IR) department at Washington University in St. Louis, I had the privilege of working under Dr. Sasha and other leading specialists including but not limited to Dr. Mani, Dr. Atta, Dr. Azhar, Dr. Kim and Dr. Berry-Tony. I gained hands-on experience with a variety of advanced IR techniques, including CT-guided biopsies, CVC and PICC line placements, angioplasties, and IVC filter placements. Additionally, I closely observed procedures like uterine artery embolization,embolization for GI bleeding, Sclerotherapy for congential and acquired anamolies, Radiofrequency and Microwave Ablation, percutaneous drainage procedures for abscesses, percutaneous transhepatic cholangiography and biliary drain placements and Y-90 radioembolization for liver cancer patients further deepening my understanding of vascular interventions. Engaged in post-procedure patient care, including monitoring patients for immediate complications, reviewing post-procedure imaging for treatment efficacy, and following up with long-term outcomes. Attended morning rounds and noon conferences to learn about the future of IR and medical education in IR. Also, loved exploring the university city and forest park was my favourite go to place!

Clinical Observer (Interventional Radiology) Lenox Hill Hospital Under the supervision of Chief of IR dept. Dr. Mark A. Westcott, Dr. Kirk, and Dr. Art, I observed and learned about a range of advanced procedures, including Y-90 radioembolization, prostate artery embolization, uterine artery embolization, Flouroscopic guided Biopsies and thrombectomies. Additionally, I gained hands-on experience in patient consultations, pre- and post-procedure care, and imaging techniques like fluoroscopy and ultrasound guidance. From Morning rounds to Noon Conferences alongside learning how to use EMR, this observership not only deepened my understanding of cutting-edge IR techniques but also reinforced my commitment to pursuing a career in this dynamic specialty.
