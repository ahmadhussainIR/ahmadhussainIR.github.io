Research Interests

  • Imaging biomarkers and integration of imaging phenotypes with clinical and molecular data

Research Experience

I am currently working as a Clinical Research Fellow in the Department of Interventional Radiology at Brigham and Women’s Hospital / Harvard Medical School, where I am actively involved in clinical trials and translational research focused on embolization therapies. My work includes serving as a co-investigator and research coordinator for ongoing trials such as the ELECTRC embolization trial and the PCN trial. I am involved in patient screening, eligibility assessment, procedural planning, and angiographic interpretation, with a focus on evaluating embolization endpoints and treatment response. I also perform quantitative imaging analysis using techniques such as digital subtraction angiography (DSA), angiographic blush assessment, and time–density curve analysis. I utilize tools including ImageJ/FIJI and 3D Slicer for image processing, segmentation, and perfusion analysis to better understand treatment efficacy and optimize patient selection. My prior research experience includes work as a research assistant at LUMS, where I investigated immune dysfunction in type 2 diabetes and its role in increased susceptibility to infectious diseases. In addition, I have laboratory experience in histopathology, tissue engineering, genetics, and molecular biology techniques, including PCR, ELISA, and flow cytometry. My research aims to develop more precise, imaging-driven approaches to patient selection and outcome prediction in embolization therapies.
